The pn junction cannot made by simply by joining two pieces. Special fabrication techniques are required to form a pn junction. Breakdown mechanism of the diode

IF the reverse bias is applied to the diode then it increased to a point where the junction breaks down and reverse current rise sharply to a value limited only by the external resistance coneected.
  1. Zener breakdown
    When the diode is reverse biased the energy of electrons increases and they move at high velocity.
    The process in which electrons moves across the barrier from the valance band of the P to the conduction band of N type.
    This is observed in zener diode having a zener breakdown voltage Vz of 5 to 8 volt.
    The valence electrons are cooled in the conduction due to high electric field in the narrow depletion region. The increase in breakdown decrease the breakdown voltage of the diode.
    In VI characteristics it takes a sharp curve and is heavily dopped.
  2. Avelanche Breakdown
    The process of applying high voltage and increasing the electric current in semiconductor will lead to instant breakdown. This is observed in Zener diode having a avelanche breakdown Vz voltage that is 8 volt or above.
    The valence electrons pushed to a conduction imparted energy accelerated by which electrons gain their velocity due to their collision with other atoms.
    The increase in temperature increase the breakdown voltage. VI characteristics is gradually decreasing. Doping is very less or light.
